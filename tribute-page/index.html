20.10.20

End police brutality

Here is a timeline of the events leading to 20.10.20:

  • 30.03.20 - Lockdown announced in all major cities in Nigeria
  • 28.09.20 - Planned strike by the Nigeria Labour Congress (NLC) got cancelled
  • 04.10.20 - Resurgence of #EndSARS trend on twitter
  • 08.10.20 - #EndSARS protests kicked off in all major cities
  • 11.10.20 - Government accedes to demands of protesters (#5for5); disbands SARS, initiates process to introduce SWAT
  • 13.10.20 - Backlash from SWAT decision causes protests to intensify across the country, especially in sit-in locations like the Lekki Tollgate
  • 20.10.20 16:00(GMT +1) - Lagos State Government imposes curfew to restore order
  • 20.10.20 18:45(GMT +1) - Soldiers arrive, massacred defenceless protesters with live ammunition

"Protests at the Lekki Toll Gate on 20 October were part of the #EndSARS protests that began on 8 October 2020. Nigerians have been taking to the streets, peacefully demanding an end to police brutality, extrajudicial executions and extortion by the Special Anti-Robbery Squad (SARS), a unit of the Nigerian police tasked with fighting violent crimes. But they have been met with excessive use of force by the army and police forces.
On 20 October peaceful protesters were reportedly shot dead when the Nigerian Army opened fire on protesters at the Lekki Toll Gate."

-- Amnesty International, 2020
